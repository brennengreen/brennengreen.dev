I am a computer science student interested in the domain of computer graphics. I am inspired by the intersection of art and technology, specifically for the domain of film and games. I am particularly interested in the topics of rendering, animation, game engines, gpu architecture, and GPGPU computing. I've fulfilled this interest so far in multiple ways: Through my experience at Twitch where I had ownership of a brand new tool which allowed for the rendering of dynamic stream content on streams. Through several research projects around the topics of computer vision and machine learning GPGPU problems in biomedical and process optimization fields. And most recently at Blizzard Entertainment working on the World of Warcraft Engine Team, helping to create a modern, compute-based, particle system which will enable vfx artist to create high-fidelty, stunning particle-effects for the game.

Compuer Graphics is my passion, and I love getting my hands dirty in the low-level details of hardware implementation and how it can be used to enable the performance and accessibility of systems accross games, movies, and the ever increasing world of GPGPU programming.

When I'm not obsessing over computer graphics however, you can find me playing games, cooking, skating, surfing or doing anything outdoors!
